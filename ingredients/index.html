Why is there so much grain?

In simple terms, the brewing process for beer works as follows:

1

We start with “the mash” where water is run through grain

This creates “wort” - sugary water

2

The wort is put into a Kettle with some hops and brought to the boil

The hops give flavour, aroma and bitterness

3

The wort is then cooled and put in a fermenting vessel with some yeast

The yeast eats the sugar and creates CO2 and alcohol

4

The beer is then often left to condition

It can soon be packaged into casks, kegs, bottles or cans!

It takes about 1kg of grain to produce 10 pints of beer. Breweries across Europe produce around 3.4million tonnes of “spent grain” a year.
